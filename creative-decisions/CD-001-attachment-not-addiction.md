## The decision

ERA's North Star is **Attachment** — a Club the player never wants to leave
behind. The strongest long-term emotion ERA seeks is **attachment, not
addiction.** Progression, optimization, collection and competition are *means*
that matter only because they serve it — never the purpose.

## Alternatives considered

- **Addiction / engagement-maximization** — "why is this game addictive" (the
  proposal chat's early framing); optimize for compulsion and time-on-device.
- **Progression as the purpose** — levels, upgrades, currencies as the point.
- **Optimization / mastery** as the end in itself.
- **Collection** as the end.
- **Competition** as the end.

## Why this won

The four "means" alternatives all describe things ERA *contains* — but none of
them explains *why they matter* or why the Club should outlive any single season.
Only attachment does: progression, winning, collecting and mastering are
emotionally meaningful **because** they accrue to something the player has come to
care about. Attachment is the frame under which every other emotion becomes load-
bearing.

Addiction fails on its own terms for this game. Engineered compulsion optimizes
for time-on-device and decays into resentment; it burns the very thing — a place
you love and *choose* to return to — that makes ERA distinct. Attachment
optimizes instead for a Club worth returning to for *years*, which is the actual
ambition. And only attachment makes sense of ERA's most distinctive design moves:
a world that continues without the player, honest loss that leaves scars, and a
place you would genuinely *miss*. None of those are legible under an addiction
frame; all are inevitable under an attachment frame.

## What it rejects or supersedes

- **Rejects** the "addictive" framing from the proposal chat as a *design goal*
  (a genuine contradiction in the source material, now resolved in attachment's
  favour).
- **Rejects** engagement-maximization as ERA's optimization target.

## Consequences

- ERA must **never weaponize attachment into addiction** — this becomes a
  candidate "negative promise" (IP-001 OQ-7).
- The **Ultimate Design Question** ("does this help players build a Club they'll
  never want to leave behind?") becomes the feature filter.
- Monetization must not damage attachment; anything that trades long-term
  attachment for short-term compulsion is out of bounds.
