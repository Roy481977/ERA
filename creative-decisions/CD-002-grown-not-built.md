## The decision

The root metaphor for the player–Club relationship is **"a Club is grown, not
built"** — replacing **"the Club as Partner."** *(The metaphor's final wording is
held open — AT-4 is a candidate — but the rejection of "partner" is decided.)*

## Links

- **Import Packages:** IP-002 AT-4 (candidate root metaphor), RJ-1 ("Club as
  Partner" rejected), and it feeds CD-005 / axiom AR. IP-001 AT-5 (the player owns
  direction; the Club owns culture).

## Alternatives considered

- **"The Club as Partner."**
- **The Club as possession / tool** (fully controlled).
- **The Club as a mirror** of the player (fully passive).

## Why this won

"Partner" implies **equality** — a symmetric counterpart that negotiates as an
equal. The relationship isn't that: it is **asymmetric and organic.** The player
shapes the Club; the Club grows into something beyond the player's intention.
"Grown" carries time, emergence, culture, scars and surprise without pretending
the Club is the player's equal. The two other alternatives fail at the extremes:
*possession/tool* is too controlling (a sterile object that only executes intent),
and *mirror* is too passive (no independence, nothing to be surprised by).
"Grown, not built" sits where attachment actually lives — **cultivation, not
authorship.**

## What it rejects or supersedes

- **Rejects** RJ-1, "the Club as Partner" (implies equality).

## Consequences

- Establishes the gardener framing: the player owns direction and tending, not
  outcomes; culture emerges. Directly feeds the Relational Axiom (CD-005).
- Justifies "the world continues without the player" and honest, un-chosen loss.
