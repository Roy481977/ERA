## The decision

**Meaning and Recognition are two distinct axioms.** *Meaning* is narrative and
can be wholly solitary; *Recognition* is social and requires another mind.

## Links

- **Import Packages:** IP-002 A3 (Meaning), A2 (Recognition), CE-3 (the
  compression: Meaning separated from Recognition), CR-2.

## Alternatives considered

- **"Meaning is fundamentally social"** — the CKO's earlier fused axiom, "Meaning
  is social and narrative," which bundled the two together.
- **Collapsing Meaning into Recognition** as one thing.

## Why this won

Recognition has an **irreducible second-person structure**: it must come from
outside, because a regard you generate for yourself is just self-esteem — you
cannot recognize yourself into mattering. Meaning has **no such requirement**:
people find profound meaning alone — the diary, the cabin, the planted tree — and
narrative is witnessed, if only by one's *future self*. The clean test that
divides them: *can it survive with no other mind, ever?* **Meaning passes;
Recognition fails by definition.** The split also cleanly re-sorts the two
memories — **player → Club memory = Meaning** (narrative, solitary); **Club →
player memory = Recognition** (needs the other mind).

## What it rejects or supersedes

- **Rejects** the fused "Meaning is social and narrative" axiom; separates it into
  two.

## Consequences

- Two distinct axioms in the set (alongside the Relational axiom).
- The memory model is re-cleaved along the Meaning/Recognition line.
- In the living world: solitary meaning (the player's own remembered Club) is
  distinct from being recognized by the world (Victor/Emma/Nora).
