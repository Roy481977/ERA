## Intent

This is the first entry in ERA's institutional memory. It captures the
**evolution of ERA's worldview** as three things: the truths we converged on,
the questions still open, and the ideas we deliberately rejected. Brainstorming
and conversational back-and-forth are intentionally excluded; only decisions and
live questions are preserved. On Roy's ratification, the Accepted Truths become
the canonical foundation the Constitution is written to protect.

## A. Accepted Truths — recommended → CANONICAL (pending ratification)

Each is a converged philosophical decision. "Home" is the recommended canonical
document (see the Placement Map in §D). Where an idea evolved, the lineage is
noted.

**AT-1 — The North Star is Attachment.**
ERA exists to allow every football fan to build a Club they will never want to
leave behind. Progression, optimization, collection and competition are not the
purpose; they matter only because they serve attachment. The strongest
long-term emotion ERA seeks is attachment, not addiction.
*Home:* `constitution/north-star`.

**AT-2 — The Core Loop.**
Real football creates emotion → fantasy transforms that emotion into meaningful
consequences → the Club preserves those consequences → time transforms them into
identity, memories and legacy. Players are not building save files; they are
building a living Club with a life of its own.
*Home:* `constitution/core-loop`.

**AT-3 — The Ultimate Design Question.**
Every feature must answer: *"Does this help players build a Club they will never
want to leave behind?"* If yes, it belongs; if not, it is challenged.
*Home:* `constitution/north-star`.

**AT-4 — Root metaphor: a Club is grown, not built.**
Nothing meaningful in football appears fully formed, and neither should a Club.
Growth encompasses time, emergence, culture, scars, traditions, surprise,
stewardship and attachment. This is the unifying image beneath the whole
worldview. *(Note: reconciles with the word "build" in AT-1/AT-3 as — the player
builds in the sense of choosing and tending; the Club grows beyond the player's
intention. Wording to be harmonized when canonical docs are written. Its tension
with football's competitiveness is tracked as OQ-3.)*
*Lineage:* replaced the "Club as Partner" metaphor (see RJ-1).
*Home:* `constitution/grown-not-built`.

**AT-5 — Authorship is split: the player owns direction, the Club owns culture.**
The player always owns **direction** — ambitions, investments, philosophy,
strategy. The Club gradually owns **culture** — traditions, memories, supporters,
rivalries, legends, identity. Culture *emerges* because enough has happened that
it becomes inevitable; it is never invented by the AI. "The player builds the
Club; the Club develops a personality of its own." The player remains the
primary author of *intention*, not of every *outcome*.
*Home:* `constitution/authorship`.

**AT-6 — Reality is the Club's co-author.**
Reality continuously writes parts of the Club's story that neither the player nor
the designers completely control. This is one of ERA's defining constitutional
ideas. It is stated as a *philosophical* truth only; the Constitution does **not**
bind itself to any implementation of it (licensing, APIs, data feeds, commercial
partnerships are architecture and may evolve — see RJ-3).
*Home:* `constitution/authorship`.

**AT-7 — History is emotionally honest: it remembers both glory and pain.**
Nothing meaningful is permanent, and everything meaningful leaves a trace. Some
moments should permanently hurt; some seasons should stay painful; some
rivalries should never completely heal; some departures should still be spoken
about years later. The goal is not punishment but significance and honesty. Not
every setback is redeemed into a pleasant chapter — some traces are scars.
*Lineage:* corrects the earlier "loss should rarely feel like wasted effort /
every setback eventually becomes another story" framing (see RJ-2).
*Home:* `constitution/honest-history`.

**AT-8 — Significance comes from investment, not from punishment.**
Meaningful memories require something to obtain — football knowledge, time,
commitment, creativity, patience, financial investment, luck, community
participation, or combinations. Setbacks are healthy and become part of the
Club's biography (subject to AT-7: some remain scars).
*Home:* `constitution/honest-history`.

**AT-9 — Two kinds of memory, both preserved.**
*Historic Memory* = the moments players would proudly tell another person years
later (titles, legendary matches, heroes, defining rivalries) → creates **pride**.
*Living Memory* = the things nobody tells stories about but everyone would miss
if they vanished (the familiar groundskeeper, the pre-match music, the stadium
cat, recurring faces and rituals) → creates **belonging**. History creates pride;
familiarity creates belonging. Both deserve preservation.
*Home:* `constitution/memory`.

**AT-10 — Belonging emerges; it cannot be awarded.**
Belonging cannot be unlocked or granted. It accumulates through repeated shared
experience — the shift from "this is where I…" to "this is where we…". It is
built from continuity: recurring rituals, anniversaries, shared celebrations and
disappointments, recognizable faces and places, and a world (supporters, AI)
that remembers history.
*Home:* `constitution/belonging`.

**AT-11 — The Human Test governs authenticity.**
Every emergent or AI-generated behavior must pass one question: *"Would a real
football supporter believe this could naturally happen?"* If not, it does not
belong. AI must never feel like an author; it should feel like reality
unfolding. The more invisible the AI, the more successful it is. *(Accepted as
the bar for authenticity. Note: authenticity is necessary but does not settle
presence — see OQ-2.)*
*Home:* `constitution/belonging` (or `constitution/the-human-test`).

**AT-12 — Richness is emotional depth, not decoration.**
Every meaningful detail exists to deepen the player's relationship with the Club
— because it generates feeling, not because it generates gameplay. The test for
any detail: *"Does this make the Club feel more alive, more personal and more
worth caring about?"* Expression (weather, supporters, architecture, music,
traditions, artifacts, and countless small details) is flexible; the intention
is constant.
*Home:* `constitution/richness`.

**AT-13 — The Four Emotional Clocks.**
ERA creates emotion across overlapping timescales that reinforce one another:
Match Clock (minutes — anticipation, hope, tension, celebration, heartbreak);
Club Clock (days/weeks — discovery, ownership, care, progress, satisfaction);
Season Clock (months — ambition, pride, recognition, reflection, belonging);
Legacy Clock (years — identity, stewardship, nostalgia, continuity, legacy).
Every meaningful feature should strengthen one or more clocks.
*Home:* `constitution/emotional-clocks`.

**AT-14 — The Club is never finished.**
Players should not merely build a bigger Club; they should continuously
rediscover it. Every season adds another layer of life, story and tradition. The
Club keeps growing in emotional richness across its entire lifetime.
*Home:* `constitution/the-unfinished-club`.

**AT-15 — Persistent layers outlive generations.**
Players come and go; the Club remains. Identity persists across four layers:
Physical World (stadium, training ground, academy, museum, memorials, statues,
architecture, neighborhoods, landscape); Supporters (culture, traditions, chants,
loyalty, demographics, rivalries, reputation); Institutional Identity
(philosophy, style, values, academy reputation, transfer philosophy, financial
character, global reputation); Historic Memory (trophies, eras, legends, iconic
matches, anniversaries, artifacts). AI should gradually *expand* these layers, so
the Club becomes increasingly alive — not merely more complicated.
*Home:* `constitution/the-unfinished-club`.

**AT-16 — The Emotional Pillars (content accepted; structure open).**
The emotional outcomes every major system should reinforce: Ownership,
Competition, Attachment, Wonder, Pride, Stewardship, Legacy. These are accepted
as the emotional targets. Their *structure* is not yet settled (Attachment is
also the North Star; Ownership/Pride and Stewardship/Legacy overlap; Competition
is arguably an engine rather than an outcome) — see OQ-6.
*Home:* `constitution/emotional-pillars`.

## B. Open Constitutional Questions — → OPEN

These are live forks the Constitution cannot be finalized without deciding.

**OQ-2 — Presence vs. authenticity (the AI-vs-human question).**
The Human Test (AT-11) guarantees that emergent behavior *feels* real, but not
that anyone else is actually present. An entire supporter culture can pass every
Human Test and still leave the player as the only real person in the room.
Decision needed: is authentic simulation *sufficient* for belonging, or does
ERA's "we" require real human presence (other players)? This changes the entire
emotional and technical architecture and is the single largest open question.

**OQ-3 — Does "grown, not built" under-serve football's competitiveness?**
Cultivation is peaceful; football is contested, cruel and often unfair — the
original philosophy called Competition "the heartbeat." How does the root
metaphor (AT-4) hold *the storm* as well as the garden, so ERA does not drift
cozy? (Candidate reconciliation: a Club grows in a *contested climate* where
rivals compete for the same light and a storm can take a season — not yet
adopted.)

**OQ-4 — The arrow of real time / stepping away.**
Because reality co-authors (AT-6), the Club grows even when the player is absent;
returning after a long absence means finding a Club reshaped by football the
player never witnessed. Is this a poignant strength or an alienation? The
returning-player experience must be designed deliberately. (Philosophical, not
architectural.)

**OQ-5 — Newcomers vs. veterans.**
A Club "increasingly difficult to fully understand" (AT-15) is alive to its
veteran and potentially impenetrable to a newcomer. If belonging requires shared
history (AT-10), newcomers start outside it. Does ERA privilege the
long-tenured to the point of gatekeeping the new? (Linked to OQ-2.)

**OQ-6 — Structure of the Emotional Pillars.**
Resolve the taxonomy: Attachment as North Star vs. pillar; the Ownership/Pride
and Stewardship/Legacy overlaps; Competition as engine vs. outcome. A cleaner
structure (e.g. pillars as tributaries feeding Attachment) is needed before the
Constitution formalizes them.

**OQ-7 — The negative promises.**
What does ERA constitutionally *refuse* to do to protect attachment — especially
against future monetization pressure? Candidates: never weaponize attachment into
addiction; never erase or reset history; never let the Club be trivially
destroyed or bought past. Not yet written.

**OQ-8 — The boundary of emergent culture (can the Club "win"?).**
AT-5 resolves most of the authorship tension, but the operational edge remains:
can the Club retain a tradition, grudge or identity the player wants removed —
i.e., does emergent culture ever hold ground against the owner's wishes, and
where is that line drawn?

## C. Rejected & Superseded Ideas — → REJECTED (preserved with reasons)

Kept so they are never silently re-litigated. (These were *proposals*, never
canon, so they are classified REJECTED with a pointer to what replaced them.
Once canonical items exist, a later replacement of a canonical item would instead
be classified SUPERSEDED.)

**RJ-1 — "The Club as Partner."**
Rejected because *partner implies equality*, and the player–Club relationship is
asymmetric and organic rather than equal. Replaced by AT-4 ("grown, not built")
plus AT-5 (direction vs. culture).

**RJ-2 — Loss as always-redeemable / frictionless.**
The earlier framing — "loss should rarely feel like wasted effort; every setback
should eventually create another story" — was rejected as *too comfortable*: a
guaranteed redemptive arc removes the vulnerability attachment depends on.
Replaced by AT-7 (history remembers both glory and pain; some traces are scars).

**RJ-3 — Binding the Constitution to implementation of reality-as-co-author.**
Rejected as constitutional content: licensing, APIs, data feeds and commercial
partnerships are *architecture* and may evolve. Only the philosophical truth
(AT-6) is constitutional.

**RJ-4 — Visible AI authorship.**
Rejected as an approach: AI-generated content that reads as "the AI generated
this" fails the worldview. AI must disappear behind authenticity (AT-11).

## E. Drafted Decision Records (proposed)

Compact records of the decisions this package ratifies. Status: **proposed**;
finalized on Roy's approval.

- **DR-001 — North Star = Attachment** (AT-1, AT-3). *Context:* the game needed a
  single purpose above progression/optimization/collection/competition.
  *Decision:* attachment is the end; all else serves it. *Consequence:* the
  Ultimate Design Question becomes ERA's feature filter. *Related:* OQ-6.
- **DR-002 — Root metaphor = Grown, not Built** (AT-4). *Context:* "partner"
  implied equality and felt wrong. *Decision:* a Club is grown, never
  fully created. *Consequence:* agency-without-total-control becomes the frame
  for authorship, reality, culture and scars. *Related:* OQ-3; supersedes RJ-1.
- **DR-003 — Authorship split: player owns direction, Club owns culture**
  (AT-5). *Consequence:* culture must *emerge* from accumulated history, never be
  AI-invented. *Related:* OQ-8.
- **DR-004 — Reality is co-author (philosophy only)** (AT-6). *Decision:* enshrine
  the truth, not its implementation. *Consequence:* ERA is philosophically
  coupled to a world it cannot fully control. *Related:* OQ-4; excludes RJ-3.
- **DR-005 — History is emotionally honest** (AT-7, AT-8). *Decision:* permanent
  scars are allowed; significance comes from investment, not punishment.
  *Related:* corrects RJ-2.
- **DR-006 — Two kinds of memory** (AT-9). *Decision:* preserve Historic Memory
  (pride) and Living Memory (belonging) as distinct.
- **DR-007 — The Human Test** (AT-11). *Decision:* authenticity is the bar for all
  emergent/AI behavior; AI stays invisible. *Related:* OQ-2 (authenticity ≠
  presence).
