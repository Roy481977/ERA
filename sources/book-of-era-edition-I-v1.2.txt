Edition I – The Foundation
Version 1.2 – The District Awakens
Constitutional Discoveries
• The stage comes before the actors. Places are designed before lives.
• The district is remembered through routes, not coordinates.
• Life follows geography. Every resident has territory, rhythms and habits.
• The district is a theatre without an audience. Life never performs for the player.
• The player never fully owns the district. Roughly 20–30% should always remain beyond view.
• Design places before stories. Stories emerge from places.
• Living Density: the player should constantly feel that fifty things are happening that have nothing to do with them.
District Identity
ERA is not a city and not a map. It is a football district that has naturally grown around the Club over more than a century. The stadium is the heart, but life is visually and emotionally larger than football.
Emotional Geography
• The Club: Heart of the district. Always visible but never dominant.
• Main Street: Emotional spine. Bakery, florist, café, pub, museum, independent shops. Nearly everyone passes here.
• Plaza: Social heart. Fountain, celebrations, markets, memorials, children, musicians.
• Museum Quarter: Memory. Quiet, reflective, slower.
• Riverside: Silence, foxes, walks, old bridge, ancient oak.
• Academy: Hope. Children, parents, dreams.
• Railway: Arrival, departure, visitors, journalists, away supporters.
Camera Philosophy
The player looks into a living miniature world rather than a strategy map.

Zoom 1 – District: understand mood, weather, crowds and rhythm.
Zoom 2 – Street: recognize routines and familiar faces.
Zoom 3 – Life: observe conversations, tiny moments, leaves, rain, expressions.
District Composition
• Stadium in lower-right quadrant, occupying roughly 20–25% of the composition.
• Main Street occupies the visual center (~40%).
• Parks, river, academy and edges occupy the remaining ~35%.
• Stadium is always visible but not visually dominant.
• Main Street runs diagonally from railway station toward stadium.
• Plaza with fountain sits between stadium and Main Street.
• River and old stone bridge define one edge.
• Residential neighborhoods continue beyond the visible frame.
• The world should never feel fully contained on screen.

Daily Rhythms
Morning: bakery, station, groundskeeper, dog, deliveries.
Lunch: cafés, florist, museum.
Afternoon: academy, park, children.
Evening: pub, couples, Main Street.
Night: fox, cats, street cleaners, wind.
Match Day
The district changes personality rather than simply increasing population.
Street vendors, scarves, music, police horses, journalists, overflowing station, bakery at full production, excited dog, disappearing cats, singing plaza. After the match the town slowly exhales back into silence.
