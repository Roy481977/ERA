# Vision

> "Build worlds that people believe existed before they arrived and will continue after they leave."

---

## The Long-Term Vision

ERA is a platform for living worlds.

Each world is a continuously evolving society populated by autonomous individuals whose lives emerge from simulation rather than scripts.

Football Town is only the first expression of this technology.

Future worlds may explore different cultures, time periods and themes, but they should all share the same foundation:

People.

Places.

Time.

Memory.

Culture.

---

## Living Worlds

A living world is not defined by AI.

Nor by graphics.

Nor by scale.

A living world is one where every day is the natural consequence of yesterday.

The past continuously shapes the future.

Nothing exists in isolation.

---

## A Town That Breathes

The town should never feel like a stage waiting for the player.

Morning exists before the player wakes.

Workers begin their routines.

Children leave for school.

Shops open.

The groundskeeper prepares the pitch.

Rain changes plans.

Someone arrives late.

Someone stays home.

The player is entering an existing world.

Not activating one.

---

## Time

Time is one of ERA's primary simulation systems.

Days become weeks.

Weeks become seasons.

Seasons become years.

Nothing should permanently reset.

Life accumulates.

Buildings age.

Trees grow.

Children become adults.

Players retire.

Memories fade.

New traditions emerge.

The passage of time should be visible.

---

## The Importance of Football

Football is not merely entertainment.

Football is the social heartbeat of the first world.

It determines:

- weekly rhythms
- emotional highs and lows
- conversations
- rivalries
- celebrations
- disappointments
- local identity
- family traditions
- business activity

Removing football should fundamentally change the town.

---

## The Human Scale

ERA is intentionally designed around human observation.

The player should eventually recognize individuals.

Not because they memorized a database.

Because they lived alongside them.

Recognition is more important than quantity.

---

## Believability

Believability does not require realism.

The simulation should simplify reality whenever simplification produces a clearer and more understandable world.

The objective is not scientific accuracy.

The objective is internal consistency.

Players should gradually learn how this world works.

Once learned, the world should rarely surprise them without reason.

Unexpected events should emerge from understandable causes.

---

## Stories

Stories are not authored.

Stories emerge.

No system should exist solely to generate narrative.

Narrative is a consequence of simulation.

A memorable story is evidence that the simulation is working.

---

## Identity

Every town should gradually develop an identity.

Not because designers assign one.

Because years of accumulated history create one.

One town may become known for producing great goalkeepers.

Another for always celebrating in the square.

Another for generations of bakers.

Identity should emerge.

Never be declared.

---

## Emotional Success

The highest compliment a player can give ERA is not:

"The AI is impressive."

It is:

"I missed Victor."

Or:

"I always stop by the Oak before heading to the stadium."

Or:

"It doesn't feel like NPCs anymore."

When residents become people in the player's mind, ERA has achieved its purpose.

---

## North Star

Every major design decision should answer one question:

Will this help players believe this world is truly alive?

If not, reconsider it.

No matter how technically impressive the feature may be.
