# ERA — The Living World: Residents

**Status: PROPOSED.** The residents system, consolidated from the Book of ERA
(`sources/book-of-era-edition-I-v1.2`). The canonical draft resident list and the
resident design template are Roy's; the **filled example biography is marked
_[CKO-proposed draft]_** — written to fill the open question "expand each
resident into a complete biography," for Roy to approve, edit, or replace.

Residents are not NPCs with quests. They are **inhabitants with territory,
rhythms and habits** — the district is a *theatre without an audience*, and they
never perform for the player. Attachment to them forms the way it forms to real
familiar faces: through recurrence, not interaction. *(This is IP-002 A3 Meaning
via familiarity, and the Living-Memory half of IP-001 AT-9 — the people you'd
miss if they vanished, though you never tell a story about them.)*

## Resident design template

Every resident is defined by the same fields, so the cast stays coherent:

- **Essence** — the one-line soul of the resident.
- **Biography** — who they are and how they came to the district.
- **Daily Rhythm** — where they are across morning/lunch/afternoon/evening/night.
- **Match Day** — how they change when the district changes personality.
- **Seasonal Behaviour** — how they shift across the football year and weather.
- **Relationships** — their ties to other residents and places.
- **Things They Notice** — what they react to in the world.
- **Things They Never Notice** — their blind spots.
- **Things That Slowly Change** — what time does to them.
- **Things That Never Change** — their constants (the anchor of familiarity).
- **Conversations About Them** — what others in the district say (a recognition
  channel: the world *remembers* and *discusses* them — IP-002 A2).
- **What The Player May Never Learn** — deliberately withheld depth (~20–30%
  beyond view).
- **Never** — hard rules; what this resident must never do.
- **Visual Direction** — how they read on screen.

## Example biography — The Old Dog  _[CKO-proposed draft]_

A worked example of the template, written to show how a resident fleshes out.
**Proposed for Roy's approval/editing — not canon.**

- **Essence** — the district's oldest constant; the heartbeat you only notice
  when it's gone.
- **Biography** — no one is quite sure who the Old Dog belongs to, which is the
  point: he belongs to the district. He has slept on the bakery step for as long
  as the morning crowd can remember. Older supporters swear he was there for the
  last promotion; the young ones assume he always was.
- **Daily Rhythm** — Morning: the bakery step, waiting for the first delivery.
  Lunch: the shade by the café tables. Afternoon: trailing the children toward
  the academy pitches. Evening: the pub doorway. Night: gone — no one knows where.
- **Match Day** — transformed. He is up early, agitated and delighted, weaving
  through the vendors and scarves, barking at the police horses, drawn to the
  singing plaza. When the town exhales back into silence after the final whistle,
  he is the last thing moving on Main Street.
- **Seasonal Behaviour** — slower and stiffer in deep winter; he keeps to the
  bakery's warm doorway. In the first warm evening of a new season he reappears
  on the plaza as if summoned.
- **Relationships** — the baker leaves the first broken roll out for him without
  comment. The children think he is theirs. The cats vanish when he arrives.
- **Things They Notice** — the delivery van, the children, the smell of match day.
- **Things They Never Notice** — trophies, results, the player.
- **Things That Slowly Change** — his pace; the grey around his muzzle; how long
  he sleeps.
- **Things That Never Change** — the bakery step. He is *always* on the bakery
  step in the morning. This is the anchor.
- **Conversations About Them** — "He was here before any of us." "Someone should
  find out whose he is." "It won't be the same when he's gone."
- **What The Player May Never Learn** — where he goes at night; whose he is;
  how old he really is.
- **Never** — never begs, never performs for the player, never reacts to menus or
  UI, never disappears without the world noticing.
- **Visual Direction** — a soft, low, unhurried shape; warm brown; painterly, not
  cute. He should read as *old and loved*, never as a mascot.
