# ERA — The Living World: Residents

**Status: PROPOSED.** The residents system, consolidated from the Book of ERA
(`sources/book-of-era-edition-I-v1.2`). The canonical draft resident list and the
resident design template are Roy's; the **filled example biography is marked
_[CKO-proposed draft]_** — written to fill the open question "expand each
resident into a complete biography," for Roy to approve, edit, or replace.

Residents are not NPCs with quests. They are **inhabitants with territory,
rhythms and habits** — the district is a *theatre without an audience*, and they
never perform for the player. Attachment to them forms the way it forms to real
familiar faces: through recurrence, not interaction. *(This is IP-002 A3 Meaning
via familiarity, and the Living-Memory half of IP-001 AT-9 — the people you'd
miss if they vanished, though you never tell a story about them.)*

## Resident design template

Every resident is defined by the same fields, so the cast stays coherent:

- **Essence** — the one-line soul of the resident.
- **Biography** — who they are and how they came to the district.
- **Daily Rhythm** — where they are across morning/lunch/afternoon/evening/night.
- **Match Day** — how they change when the district changes personality.
- **Seasonal Behaviour** — how they shift across the football year and weather.
- **Relationships** — their ties to other residents and places.
- **Things They Notice** — what they react to in the world.
- **Things They Never Notice** — their blind spots.
- **Things That Slowly Change** — what time does to them.
- **Things That Never Change** — their constants (the anchor of familiarity).
- **Conversations About Them** — what others in the district say (a recognition
  channel: the world *remembers* and *discusses* them — IP-002 A2).
- **What The Player May Never Learn** — deliberately withheld depth (~20–30%
  beyond view).
- **Never** — hard rules; what this resident must never do.
- **Visual Direction** — how they read on screen.

## Example biography — The Old Dog  _[CKO-proposed draft]_

A worked example of the template, written to show how a resident fleshes out.
**Proposed for Roy's approval/editing — not canon.**

- **Essence** — the district's oldest constant; the heartbeat you only notice
  when it's gone.
- **Biography** — no one is quite sure who the Old Dog belongs to, which is the
  point: he belongs to the district. He has slept on the bakery step for as long
  as the morning crowd can remember. Older supporters swear he was there for the
  last promotion; the young ones assume he always was.
- **Daily Rhythm** — Morning: the bakery step, waiting for the first delivery.
  Lunch: the shade by the café tables. Afternoon: trailing the children toward
  the academy pitches. Evening: the pub doorway. Night: gone — no one knows where.
- **Match Day** — transformed. He is up early, agitated and delighted, weaving
  through the vendors and scarves, barking at the police horses, drawn to the
  singing plaza. When the town exhales back into silence after the final whistle,
  he is the last thing moving on Main Street.
- **Seasonal Behaviour** — slower and stiffer in deep winter; he keeps to the
  bakery's warm doorway. In the first warm evening of a new season he reappears
  on the plaza as if summoned.
- **Relationships** — the baker leaves the first broken roll out for him without
  comment. The children think he is theirs. The cats vanish when he arrives.
- **Things They Notice** — the delivery van, the children, the smell of match day.
- **Things They Never Notice** — trophies, results, the player.
- **Things That Slowly Change** — his pace; the grey around his muzzle; how long
  he sleeps.
- **Things That Never Change** — the bakery step. He is *always* on the bakery
  step in the morning. This is the anchor.
- **Conversations About Them** — "He was here before any of us." "Someone should
  find out whose he is." "It won't be the same when he's gone."
- **What The Player May Never Learn** — where he goes at night; whose he is;
  how old he really is.
- **Never** — never begs, never performs for the player, never reacts to menus or
  UI, never disappears without the world noticing.
- **Visual Direction** — a soft, low, unhurried shape; warm brown; painterly, not
  cute. He should read as *old and loved*, never as a mascot.

## Resident biographies  _[CKO-proposed drafts]_

Full template biographies for the rest of the cast, written to fill the open
question. **Roles/identities are CKO-proposed** — grounded in the district's
places and rhythms and cross-linked to each other — for Roy to approve, edit, or
replace. Each quietly instantiates the attachment mechanisms (Living Memory,
Recognition, honest history, independence) noted where relevant.

### Daniel & Sofia — the couple
- **Essence** — the district's ongoing love story; the bench that is "theirs."
- **Biography** — they met on Main Street years ago; the district has watched
  them court, quarrel and return. Everyone has an opinion on whether they'll marry.
- **Daily Rhythm** — Morning apart at work; Lunch sometimes at Eva's window;
  Evening together on Main Street and the plaza bench; Night home.
- **Match Day** — they watch from the plaza together; after a great win Daniel
  finally does something bold.
- **Seasonal** — closer in autumn; a wobble in deep winter; reconciled by spring.
- **Relationships** — Eva quietly roots for them; Otto teases Daniel; the Twins spy.
- **Things They Notice** — each other, the plaza, the first warm evening.
- **Things They Never Notice** — results, standings, the player.
- **Things That Slowly Change** — their status: courting → engaged → married, over years.
- **Things That Never Change** — the bench is theirs.
- **Conversations About Them** — "Are they still together?" · "He'll never ask." ·
  "They were on that bench the night we went up."
- **What The Player May Never Learn** — whether they marry (until they do); what
  they argue about.
- **Never** — never perform for the player; never resolve on a menu; never both
  vanish at once.
- **Visual Direction** — two figures who orbit each other; body language carries
  the whole story.

### Elias — the groundskeeper
*(Living Memory; his eventual retirement is the IP-001 "beloved groundskeeper
retiring" legacy moment.)*
- **Essence** — devotion made visible in mown stripes.
- **Biography** — has tended the grounds for decades; measures his life in seasons
  of grass, not trophies. Knew the stadium before the new stand.
- **Daily Rhythm** — Morning mowing in patterns; Lunch in the shade with a flask;
  Afternoon watering and pruning; Evening checking the grounds; Night gone.
- **Match Day** — up before dawn, anxious about the surface; watches from the
  tunnel mouth, never the stands.
- **Seasonal** — fights the winter frost; proudest at the first cut of spring;
  mourns the end-of-season churn.
- **Relationships** — nods to the Old Dog; argues gently with Nora about the old
  days; the Twins fear his rake.
- **Things They Notice** — divots, weeds, the weather to come.
- **Things They Never Notice** — the score, the media, his own reputation.
- **Things That Slowly Change** — his back; the number of helpers he needs.
- **Things That Never Change** — the stripes always run the same way.
- **Conversations About Them** — "Elias has cut that grass longer than the manager's
  been alive." · "It won't be the same pitch when he's gone."
- **What The Player May Never Learn** — why he never took a day off; whether he
  ever wanted more.
- **Never** — never seeks thanks; never lets the pitch be blamed; never leaves
  mid-season.
- **Visual Direction** — a stooped figure with a slow machine; unmistakable at any zoom.

### Mrs. Hana — the baker
- **Essence** — the warmth of the district; the first light on Main Street.
- **Biography** — the bakery has been Hana's for as long as anyone has bought
  bread; she feeds the district and reads its mood by what it orders.
- **Daily Rhythm** — Morning at full production, first light; Lunch the regulars;
  Afternoon winding down; Evening cleaning; Night dark.
- **Match Day** — full production before dawn; scarves in the window; free broken
  rolls for children and the Old Dog.
- **Seasonal** — warm bread and lit windows in winter; open door in summer.
- **Relationships** — leaves the first roll for the Old Dog; mothers Luca; feuds
  fondly with Otto over whose is the district's true hearth.
- **Things They Notice** — who's sad, who's in love, who didn't come in today.
- **Things They Never Notice** — league position; her own importance.
- **Things That Slowly Change** — the menu; the queue length with the Club's fortunes.
- **Things That Never Change** — her doors open before anyone else's.
- **Conversations About Them** — "Ask Hana, she knows everyone." · "The town smells
  like Hana's on match day."
- **What The Player May Never Learn** — her past; why she never closed even in the
  lean years.
- **Never** — never turns a child away; never performs; never opens late.
- **Visual Direction** — a warm figure framed in a lit doorway; steam.

### Luca — the café owner
*(the café where retired legends sit)*
- **Essence** — the young keeper of the room where history sits down for coffee.
- **Biography** — took over the café near the ground young; didn't inherit its
  ghosts but respects them — this is where old players and supporters gather.
- **Daily Rhythm** — Morning setting up; Lunch busy (Victor's corner); Afternoon
  slow; Evening the after-work crowd; Night closing.
- **Match Day** — packed; the café becomes a second stand; Victor holds court.
- **Seasonal** — terrace tables in summer; the warm back room in winter.
- **Relationships** — keeps Victor's corner always free; Mrs. Hana mothers him;
  Emma works his tables.
- **Things They Notice** — who Victor is talking to; which regulars are missing.
- **Things They Never Notice** — that he's becoming part of the history he serves.
- **Things That Slowly Change** — his confidence; the photos on the wall.
- **Things That Never Change** — Victor's corner is always kept.
- **Conversations About Them** — "Luca keeps the corner for the old man." · "Best
  coffee near the ground."
- **What The Player May Never Learn** — whether he ever wanted to play; his own story.
- **Never** — never gives away Victor's corner; never rushes an old supporter;
  never performs.
- **Visual Direction** — young, quick, warm; always mid-motion between tables.

### Karim — the kiosk by the station
- **Essence** — the district's front page; the first face arrivals see.
- **Biography** — runs the newsstand by the railway — papers, scarves, sweets.
  Knows every arrival and every rumour before the club does.
- **Daily Rhythm** — Morning papers and commuters; Lunch quiet; Afternoon the
  school crowd; Evening last editions; Night shutters down.
- **Match Day** — scarves and flags out; away supporters and journalists throng;
  he sells out.
- **Seasonal** — umbrellas in the rain; cold drinks in the heat.
- **Relationships** — sells Emma's paper first; trades gossip with Otto; the Twins
  pilfer his sweets.
- **Things They Notice** — who arrived, who left, what the headline is.
- **Things They Never Notice** — the actual football; his own influence.
- **Things That Slowly Change** — the papers thin; more phones, fewer buyers.
- **Things That Never Change** — he opens with the first train.
- **Conversations About Them** — "Karim'll know — he knows before the club knows."
- **What The Player May Never Learn** — where he's from; why he stayed.
- **Never** — never spreads a cruel rumour; never closes on a match day; never performs.
- **Visual Direction** — a bright cluttered kiosk; a figure leaning out; papers fluttering.

### Eva — the florist
- **Essence** — the quiet keeper of the district's griefs and celebrations.
- **Biography** — the florist on Main Street; her flowers mark every wedding,
  funeral, memorial and title the district has known.
- **Daily Rhythm** — Morning deliveries and arranging; Lunch the window trade;
  Afternoon the memorial at the plaza; Evening closing; Night dark.
- **Match Day** — club colours in the window; a wreath if it's an anniversary.
- **Seasonal** — follows the true seasons most of all; bare in winter, overflowing
  in spring.
- **Relationships** — roots for Daniel & Sofia; leaves flowers at Agnes's bench;
  tends the statue garden.
- **Things They Notice** — who's buying for grief, who for love.
- **Things They Never Notice** — results; her own place in every memory.
- **Things That Slowly Change** — the memorial grows.
- **Things That Never Change** — fresh flowers at the statue every match day.
- **Conversations About Them** — "Eva did the flowers." (said at every wedding and
  every funeral)
- **What The Player May Never Learn** — her own losses.
- **Never** — never lets the memorial go bare; never performs.
- **Visual Direction** — colour spilling onto grey pavement; a careful figure.

### Milo — the plaza musician
*(IP-001 "songs emerge"; a tune that becomes tradition.)*
- **Essence** — the sound of the plaza; the district's unofficial anthem-writer.
- **Biography** — a young busker who plays the plaza; on great nights the crowd
  sings his tune and no one remembers it wasn't always theirs.
- **Daily Rhythm** — Morning elsewhere; Lunch the plaza edge; Afternoon the
  fountain; Evening the crowd; Night packing up.
- **Match Day** — the heart of the singing plaza; he leads the song.
- **Seasonal** — the arcade in winter; the fountain in summer.
- **Relationships** — the Twins are his loudest fans; Otto lets him play the pub
  doorway; Karim minds his case.
- **Things They Notice** — the crowd's mood; which song lands.
- **Things They Never Notice** — that his tune has become a tradition.
- **Things That Slowly Change** — his songs; his audience.
- **Things That Never Change** — he plays the plaza on match day.
- **Conversations About Them** — "That's Milo's song — we've always sung it."
  (though they haven't)
- **What The Player May Never Learn** — his name for the song; where he goes off-season.
- **Never** — never busks for the player; never plays a rival's anthem; never performs.
- **Visual Direction** — a small figure and an instrument against the fountain.

### Nora — the museum curator
*(Historic Memory; honest history — she never lets a scar be erased, IP-001 AT-7.)*
- **Essence** — the district's memory made deliberate; keeper of what must not be
  forgotten.
- **Biography** — keeps the club museum in the Museum Quarter; decides what the
  district remembers and how. Guards the first trophy and the retired shirts.
- **Daily Rhythm** — Morning opening the quiet halls; Lunch school tours; Afternoon
  cataloguing; Evening locking up; Night the display lights.
- **Match Day** — quieter than everywhere — the museum is where a few come to
  remember before the noise.
- **Seasonal** — anniversary exhibits; the museum marks what the calendar forgets.
- **Relationships** — argues gently with Elias about the old days; interviewed by
  Emma; Agnes is her living archive.
- **Things They Notice** — what's missing from the record; what the young don't know.
- **Things They Never Notice** — the present season.
- **Things That Slowly Change** — the collection grows; new heroes join the walls.
- **Things That Never Change** — the first trophy stays in the centre case.
- **Conversations About Them** — "Nora would know if it really happened."
- **What The Player May Never Learn** — which memory she guards most fiercely.
- **Never** — never rewrites history; never lets a scar be erased; never performs.
- **Visual Direction** — a still figure among lit cases; slow.

### Otto — the publican
- **Essence** — the district's emotional barometer; loudest in joy, heaviest in defeat.
- **Biography** — runs the supporter pub, where the district celebrates and
  grieves. Otto has poured through every era.
- **Daily Rhythm** — Morning cellar work; Lunch a few regulars; Afternoon quiet;
  Evening full; Night last orders.
- **Match Day** — the loudest place before, the truest after — roaring or silent.
- **Seasonal** — the fire in winter; the doors open in summer.
- **Relationships** — teases Daniel; feuds fondly with Mrs. Hana; lets Milo play
  the doorway; Karim's gossip lands here first.
- **Things They Notice** — the mood; who's drinking alone after a loss.
- **Things They Never Notice** — his own reputation for forgiving a tab.
- **Things That Slowly Change** — the regulars; the photos behind the bar.
- **Things That Never Change** — he opens after every match, win or lose.
- **Conversations About Them** — "Otto stayed open till three the night we went up." ·
  "He let half the town drink free after we went down."
- **What The Player May Never Learn** — how much of the district's grief he carries.
- **Never** — never gloats; never closes after a defeat; never performs.
- **Visual Direction** — a broad figure behind a warm bar; the light of the pub.

### The Twins — the children
*(the future; one may one day become the academy graduate / cult hero, IP-001.)*
- **Essence** — the district's future, tearing through its present.
- **Biography** — two inseparable kids who treat the whole district as a pitch and
  a playground. Everyone knows them; no one can tell them apart.
- **Daily Rhythm** — Morning late to school; Lunch pilfering Karim's sweets;
  Afternoon on the amateur pitches; Evening spying on Daniel & Sofia; Night home.
- **Match Day** — everywhere at once; underfoot; ecstatic.
- **Seasonal** — puddles in autumn; the river in summer; snowballs in winter.
- **Relationships** — fear Elias's rake; adore Milo; are tormented and fed by Mrs. Hana.
- **Things They Notice** — everything they shouldn't.
- **Things They Never Notice** — that they are the point of the whole thing.
- **Things That Slowly Change** — they grow; one day one of them joins the academy.
- **Things That Never Change** — they're always together.
- **Conversations About Them** — "Those two will play for us one day." · "Whose are
  they, anyway?"
- **What The Player May Never Learn** — their names; which is which.
- **Never** — never a quest-giver; never separate; never perform.
- **Visual Direction** — two small blurs of identical motion.

### Agnes — the oldest surviving supporter
*(living history and symbolic continuity; her eventual passing is a compounding
loss across all four textures — IP-002 derived model.)*
- **Essence** — the last person who remembers the beginning.
- **Biography** — the oldest supporter in the district; sits on the same bench by
  the plaza; remembers eras no one else witnessed. When she goes, a door to the
  past closes.
- **Daily Rhythm** — Morning slow to the bench; Lunch feeding pigeons; Afternoon
  in the sun; Evening helped home; Night gone.
- **Match Day** — wrapped up, at her window or the bench edge; she has seen it all before.
- **Seasonal** — rarely out in deep winter — the district worries.
- **Relationships** — Eva leaves her flowers; Nora records her memories; the Twins
  are told to "mind Agnes."
- **Things They Notice** — how much has changed; who has her father's walk.
- **Things They Never Notice** — the modern game.
- **Things That Slowly Change** — how far she can walk; how often she comes.
- **Things That Never Change** — the bench is hers.
- **Conversations About Them** — "Agnes saw the '61 side." · "We should record her
  before…"
- **What The Player May Never Learn** — the stories she never told; the season she
  never speaks of.
- **Never** — never a tutorial; never performs; her absence must always be felt.
- **Visual Direction** — a small still figure on a fixed bench; the one point that
  never moves.

### Victor — the retired legend
*(Recognition made flesh — "former players refer back to events"; his unsolicited
approval of a young player is contingent, specific recognition, IP-002 A2/AR.)*
- **Essence** — the past that still walks among them; a living statue who prefers
  coffee.
- **Biography** — a former club great who never left the district; sits daily in
  Luca's café corner. Children know his name; his goals are Nora's exhibits; his
  opinion is Emma's headline.
- **Daily Rhythm** — Morning a slow walk past the ground; Lunch his corner at
  Luca's; Afternoon at the academy fence watching the young; Evening home; Night gone.
- **Match Day** — the café holds court around him; he says little and it means
  everything.
- **Seasonal** — the anniversary of his great goal; the museum lights his cabinet.
- **Relationships** — Luca keeps his corner; Emma seeks his verdict; he watches the
  Twins with a knowing eye; nods to Agnes as an equal.
- **Things They Notice** — the young players; the shape of the team; what's been forgotten.
- **Things They Never Notice** — that he is history now.
- **Things That Slowly Change** — his walk; who remembers him unprompted.
- **Things That Never Change** — the corner is always his.
- **Conversations About Them** — "Victor thinks the kid's got it." · "Did you know
  Victor still comes every day?"
- **What The Player May Never Learn** — his regrets; the goal he'd take back.
- **Never** — never coaches the player; never trades on his fame; never performs.
- **Visual Direction** — an upright older figure with an athlete's ghost;
  unmistakable at the café corner.

### Emma — the local journalist
*(Recognition — "journalists discuss, opinions differ"; she **can be critical**,
which is why the world's regard is real: recognition must be revocable, IP-002.)*
- **Essence** — the district's voice arguing with itself; the world that discusses you.
- **Biography** — a young reporter at the local media building who covers the club
  — reports, opinions, debates, headlines. She is how the district talks about itself.
- **Daily Rhythm** — Morning chasing the story; Lunch working Luca's tables;
  Afternoon at the media building; Evening filing; Night the late edition.
- **Match Day** — everywhere with a notebook; the first draft of the district's memory.
- **Seasonal** — retrospectives at season's end; the anniversary pieces.
- **Relationships** — seeks Victor's verdict; quotes Agnes; her paper sells first
  at Karim's.
- **Things They Notice** — the story beneath the result; who's unhappy.
- **Things They Never Notice** — that she is shaping how the era will be remembered.
- **Things That Slowly Change** — her standing: from cub reporter to the district's
  chronicler.
- **Things That Never Change** — she files after every match.
- **Conversations About Them** — "Did you read what Emma wrote?" · "She was hard on
  the board this week."
- **What The Player May Never Learn** — her sources; what she chose not to print.
- **Never** — never flatters the player; **may be critical** (recognition must be
  revocable); never performs.
- **Visual Direction** — quick, notebook in hand; always at the edge of the action.
