# ERA — The Living World (The District)

**Status: PROPOSED.** This is the consolidated, deduplicated documentation of
ERA's living-world layer, integrated from three sources — the proposal ChatGPT
chat (`sources/proposal-chatgpt-chat`), the Book of ERA v1.2
(`sources/book-of-era-edition-I-v1.2`), and the Design Bible v0.1 Volumes V–VII
(`sources/design-bible-v0.1`). Faithful to those sources; **CKO-proposed
additions that fill gaps ChatGPT left are marked _[CKO-proposed]_.** Nothing here
is canonical until Roy ratifies.

Cross-references to the philosophy: several living-world principles are direct
expressions of the attachment axioms in IP-002 — noted inline.

---

## 1. What the Living World is

The Living World (the "Living Club" in the Design Bible) is **a defining system,
not decorative packaging** around the real game. It converts abstract progress,
competition, fandom and time into a *place the player can observe and enjoy*.

> **The ambition:** players should sometimes open the game with no objective
> other than to spend a minute in their club.

The player looks *into a living miniature world*, not at a strategy map.

---

## 2. Constitutional discoveries about place

From the Book of ERA. These are the laws of the living world (candidate canon):

- **The stage comes before the actors.** Places are designed before lives.
- **The district is remembered through routes, not coordinates.**
- **Life follows geography.** Every resident has territory, rhythms and habits.
- **The district is a theatre without an audience.** Life never performs for the
  player. *(= IP-002 Independence / AR: the world does not wait for the player.)*
- **The player never fully owns the district.** Roughly **20–30% should always
  remain beyond view.** *(= IP-002 AR: shape but never solely author.)*
- **Design places before stories.** Stories emerge from places.
- **Living Density:** the player should constantly feel that fifty things are
  happening that have nothing to do with them.

---

## 3. District identity

ERA is **not a city and not a map.** It is a **football district that grew
naturally around the Club over more than a century.** The stadium is the heart,
but life is visually and emotionally *larger than football*. Everything in the
district exists because football exists — every place answers the question
*"why would this naturally exist around a famous football club?"*

The district also has a **never-explained mystical root** — it is a football
place *just beside the real*, designed from the first stroke **under two moons.**
This is the source of its warmth and quiet, it threads silently through every
place, and it is **never named or discussed** in-game. See
[`the-quiet-mysteries.md`](the-quiet-mysteries.md).

## 4. Emotional geography

The district is organized emotionally, not functionally. Two complementary views
exist in the sources; both are preserved here and **_[CKO-proposed]_ they should
be unified into one canonical map (see Open Questions).**

**View A — Emotional zones** (Book of ERA):

- **The Club** — heart of the district. Always visible, never dominant.
- **Main Street** — emotional spine. Bakery, florist, café, pub, museum,
  independent shops. Nearly everyone passes here.
- **Plaza** — social heart. Fountain, celebrations, markets, memorials, children,
  musicians.
- **Museum Quarter** — memory. Quiet, reflective, slower.
- **Riverside** — silence, foxes, walks, old bridge, ancient oak.
- **Academy** — hope. Children, parents, dreams.
- **Railway** — arrival, departure, visitors, journalists, away supporters.

**View B — Concentric rings** (proposal chat):

- **Immediate ring** (places every supporter visits): Main Plaza, Statue Garden,
  Club Museum, Megastore, Ticket Office, Fan Square, Food Market, Club Café.
- **Second ring** (the operational Club): Training Centre, Academy, Women's Team,
  Medical Centre, Administration, Media Building, Broadcasting Studio.
- **Third ring** (the football town): supporter pubs, murals, apartment blocks,
  parks, local school, amateur pitches, river, train station, old industrial
  buildings, small church, market street.
- **Edges** — natural, never invisible borders: forest, sea, hills, old highway,
  railway, river. *The player never feels boxed in.*

## 5. Composition & camera

**Composition** (reconciled from Book + proposal + Roy's steer that the stadium
should be *smaller and lower-right*):

- Stadium in the **lower-right quadrant**, occupying roughly **20–25%** of the
  composition — always visible but **not visually dominant** ("the mountain
  everyone looks at," but it must not block exploration). *Evolution note: an
  earlier proposal figure of 25–30% and "dominate" was revised down per Roy.*
- **Main Street** occupies the visual centre (~40%), running **diagonally from
  the railway station toward the stadium.**
- **Plaza with fountain** sits between the stadium and Main Street.
- **River and old stone bridge** define one edge.
- Parks, river, academy and edges fill the remaining ~35%.
- Residential neighbourhoods continue **beyond the visible frame** — the world
  should never feel fully contained on screen.

**Camera** — three zooms, plus feel:

- **Zoom 1 — District:** understand mood, weather, crowds and rhythm.
- **Zoom 2 — Street:** recognize routines and familiar faces.
- **Zoom 3 — Life:** observe conversations, tiny moments, leaves, rain,
  expressions.
- Feel: slightly elevated, low isometric (~30°), close to the stadium so the
  default view shows street-level life; free scrolling; zoom in/out to focus;
  drawn, not realistic.

## 7. Daily rhythms & match day

**Daily rhythms** (the district's routine over a day):

- **Morning:** bakery, station, groundskeeper, dog, deliveries.
- **Lunch:** cafés, florist, museum.
- **Afternoon:** academy, park, children.
- **Evening:** pub, couples, Main Street.
- **Night:** fox, cats, street cleaners, wind.

**Match day** — the district *changes personality rather than simply increasing
population.* Street vendors, scarves, music, police horses, journalists, an
overflowing station, the bakery at full production, an excited dog, disappearing
cats, a singing plaza. **After the match, the town slowly exhales back into
silence** — and the grounds carry visible traces of what happened.

## 8. The world continues without the player

The club must appear to have **lived between sessions.** Staff work, visitors
arrive, construction advances, grounds are maintained, trees grow, light changes,
weather passes, supporters gather and disperse, small stories begin and end. The
world must **not** repeat one idle loop forever — it is *orchestrated* from:

institution state · local time · the real football calendar · weather · recent
results · construction · collections · supporter profile.

*(This principle is IP-002's Independence axiom made visible: the Club has its
own momentum and does not wait for the player.)*

---

## 9. Visible consequence

The living world is how the institution's state becomes *legible*. System state
maps to a world expression:

| System state | Living-world expression |
|---|---|
| Supporter growth | More visitors, fuller transport, queues, banners, denser match-day atmosphere |
| Economic strength | Maintenance quality, construction tempo, commercial activity, staffing, presentation |
| Winning | Celebrations, media presence, trophy displays, special lighting, prestige behaviour |
| Losing period | Lower attendance, quieter spaces, protest or concern — but continued loyal presence |
| Collections | Museum exhibits, statues, murals, club rooms, supporter clothing, commemorative events |
| Institutional taste | What the club displays, celebrates, preserves and invests attention in |

*(The "losing period" row — loyal presence through decline — is IP-001 AT-7
honest history / scars, and IP-002 vulnerability, expressed in the world.)*

## 10. Weather, time & the real world

The player's real-world context can supply presence (opt-in, privacy-respecting).

- **Weather layer:** clear/cloud/rain/storm/wind/heat/cold/fog/snow shape
  clothing, crowds, vegetation, surfaces, reflections, light, sound. Forecasts
  create anticipation (covers before rain, watering before heat).
- **Time layer:** local sunrise/sunset drive lighting; morning/afternoon/
  evening/night change the club; weekdays/weekends/holidays alter visitors; the
  real football schedule overrides ordinary routines on match days.
- **Fairness:** weather enriches atmosphere and light operational choices — it
  **never** determines competitive fantasy outcomes.

> **Experience rule:** the same club should feel meaningfully different at noon,
> at night, in rain, in heat, before a final, and the morning after defeat.

## 11. AI as a world-building capability

AI makes the richness standard economically achievable, but it **serves authored
design rather than replacing it** — scalable variation inside a coherent world.

- **AI may orchestrate:** timing of ambient events from world state; variation in
  crowds, clothing, signage, conversations, incidents; contextual reactions to
  results/milestones/weather/collections; personalized museum and media copy;
  procedural combinations of *approved* assets; long-tail moments that reduce
  repetition.
- **AI must not control:** fantasy scoring or objective football truth; economic
  balance (must stay deterministic and auditable); competitive outcomes;
  unreviewed brand/safety/rights-sensitive content; core visual identity without
  art-direction constraints.

> **AI principle:** use AI to multiply authored richness, not to outsource
> authorship.

*(This is the same tether as IP-002's **Human Test** (AT-11 / CR-6): everything
the world generates must read as something a real football supporter would
believe could naturally happen. The Human Test is the acceptance bar for all
AI-orchestrated living-world content.)*

## 12. Visual direction

**The world:** a warm, handcrafted, stylized isometric world — cozy, painterly,
low isometric camera (~30°), soft materials, lush greenery, a walkable football
neighbourhood. **Not realistic. Not cartoon.**

**The four rules** (proposal chat):

1. **No straight roads** unless necessary — everything curves naturally.
2. **No empty grass** — grass always contains flowers, rocks, paths, benches,
   small trees, children, dogs, supporters.
3. **Every screen has a landmark** — never generic scenery.
4. **Every landmark tells history** — not decoration, history.

**Density:** almost every screen contains movement, colour, tiny animation,
wildlife, people, things to notice. **Dense, never cluttered.**

**The Nintendo principle:** the player should always see somewhere they *want to
walk next* — a tower, a bridge, a glowing tree, a train — not because of a quest,
but because something catches the eye.

**"Come and look," not "use me":** the world should almost never scream *use me*
(no harvestable resources scattered to turn the place into a utility — an
explicit rejection of the Family Island resource model). It should whisper *come
and look.*

> **Art-direction north star:** *Create the most beautiful football place that
> has never existed, but that every supporter immediately wishes were real.*
> Gameplay, systems and progression should feel **discovered inside that place**,
> not placed on top of it.

---

## 13. Richness standard

Richness is not visual noise — it is **layered, coherent variation**. The player
should see a believable combination of macro-state and micro-detail:

- **Macro:** architecture, grounds, supporter density, construction, major events.
- **Meso:** plazas, stores, museums, training areas, traffic, queues, staff.
- **Micro:** gestures, conversations, cleaning, plant growth, birds, signage,
  changing displays, small incidents.
- **Temporal:** time of day, weekday, match schedule, season, weather, history.
- **Personal:** fandom, collections, victories, taste, economic choices, strategy.

> **Non-negotiable:** no session should feel visually abandoned. The world may be
> quiet, but it must never feel dead. *(Richness is a requirement, not a final
> polish pass.)*
