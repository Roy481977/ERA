# ERA — The Living World: Places

**Status: PROPOSED.** The district's places, defined to fill the Book of ERA's
open questions (Main Street; the plaza and fountain; the bridge, river and old
oak) and to complete the emotional geography. Faithful to the sources;
**CKO-proposed content is the writing itself** — for Roy to approve, edit, or
replace.

Two things run through every place:
- **The constitutional discoveries about place** apply everywhere (the stage
  before the actors; remembered through routes; a theatre without an audience;
  ~20–30% beyond view). See [`README.md`](README.md).
- **One quiet thread of the two moons** — seen, never said. See
  [`the-quiet-mysteries.md`](the-quiet-mysteries.md).

### Proposed map unification
The sources hold two views of the district — the **emotional zones** and the
**concentric rings** (README §4). Proposed resolution: **the emotional zones
below are the canonical spatial spine**; the rings remain a supporting *radial
reading* (how far a place sits from the stadium's gravity), not a second map.

## Place design template

Every place is defined by the same fields, so the district coheres:

**Essence · Role in the district · Composition · Daily rhythm · Match day ·
Seasonal & weather · Residents & life · Sound · What slowly changes · What never
changes · Quiet mystery · What the player may never learn · Never · Visual
direction.**

---

## The Club / Stadium — *the heart*
- **Essence** — the mountain everyone looks at; the reason the district exists.
- **Role** — the heart. Always visible, never dominant (lower-right, ~20–25%).
- **Composition** — the stadium and tunnel, the forecourt, the statue outside,
  the floodlights, Elias's pitch.
- **Daily rhythm** — quiet on ordinary days (Elias mowing, staff, tours); it
  *wakes* on match day.
- **Match day** — the district's gravity well; every street flows toward it.
- **Seasonal & weather** — floodlights against winter dusk; the surface Elias
  fights to keep.
- **Residents & life** — Elias, tour groups, Victor's slow morning walk past.
- **Sound** — a distant echo on quiet days; the roar on match day.
- **What slowly changes** — a new stand rises across years; the statue count grows.
- **What never changes** — it is always the heart; always lower-right; always
  visible but never blocking the view.
- **Quiet mystery** — on a double-moon night the floodlights seem almost
  redundant; the pitch is lit enough to play. No one turns them off to check.
- **What the player may never learn** — what lies under the oldest stand; whose
  statue is planned next.
- **Never** — never dominate the frame; never block exploration; never be the only
  thing worth looking at.
- **Visual direction** — the warm-lit mountain, pointed to by every street.

## Main Street — *the emotional spine*
- **Essence** — where nearly everyone passes; the district's daily current.
- **Role** — the connective tissue, running diagonally from the railway to the
  stadium.
- **Composition** — Hana's bakery, Eva's florist, Luca's café, Otto's pub, the
  museum edge, independent shops; it **curves**, never a straight run.
- **Daily rhythm** — Hana's dawn light → the lunch trade → evening couples →
  night street-cleaners.
- **Match day** — vendors, scarves, music, the flow toward the ground; the long
  exhale afterward.
- **Seasonal & weather** — lit and warm in winter; open doors and pavement tables
  in summer; umbrellas in rain.
- **Residents & life** — Hana, Eva, Luca, Karim's papers arriving, Daniel & Sofia.
- **Sound** — bells over shop doors, chatter, a busker's drift from the plaza.
- **What slowly changes** — shopfronts turn over; queue lengths track the Club's
  fortunes.
- **What never changes** — it always curves; it always runs railway-to-stadium;
  Hana always opens first.
- **Quiet mystery** — Main Street's light lingers a little past dusk; it is always
  the last place in the district to go dark.
- **What the player may never learn** — which shop was the district's first.
- **Never** — never a straight road; never a dead frontage; never a shop that
  exists only to be "used."
- **Visual direction** — a warm, curving, densely-alive spine aimed at the stadium.

## The Plaza & Fountain — *the social heart*
- **Essence** — where the district gathers to feel things together.
- **Role** — celebration, memorial, market, music; the public living room.
- **Composition** — the fountain at the centre; the statue garden along one edge;
  market stalls; benches (Agnes's; Daniel & Sofia's); the memorial (Eva's flowers).
- **Daily rhythm** — pigeons and Agnes in the morning; the market at lunch; Milo
  and the children through the afternoon and evening.
- **Match day** — the *singing plaza*; Milo leads; the district's joy and grief
  land here first.
- **Seasonal & weather** — the market shifts with the year; the fountain's edges
  freeze in deep winter.
- **Residents & life** — Agnes, Milo, the Twins, Eva's memorial, Daniel & Sofia.
- **Sound** — water, song, market, and — after a loss — a telling silence.
- **What slowly changes** — new memorials are added; the statue garden grows with
  the Club's legends.
- **What never changes** — the fountain is always the centre; Agnes's bench is
  always hers.
- **Quiet mystery** — on a clear night the fountain holds **two reflections** in
  its water where there should be one. No one has ever pointed it out.
- **What the player may never learn** — what coins lie at the bottom; who the
  oldest statue is of.
- **Never** — never a menu screen; never a place that only transacts; never let
  the memorial go bare.
- **Visual direction** — the bright social bowl of the district; water, light,
  gathered life.

## The Riverside — Bridge, River & Old Oak — *silence (the mystery's home)*
- **Essence** — the district's held breath; where it goes to be alone.
- **Role** — silence and memory; the counterweight to the plaza's noise.
- **Composition** — the slow river, the old stone bridge, the ancient oak, a worn
  towpath, foxes at dusk.
- **Daily rhythm** — near-empty by day; walkers and couples at dusk; the fox and
  the wind at night.
- **Match day** — untouched. The one place the match-day surge never reaches; a
  few come here precisely to be away from it.
- **Seasonal & weather** — the oak turns first; mist off the river in autumn; ice
  at the banks in winter.
- **Residents & life** — the fox, solitary walkers, the occasional grieving
  figure, Daniel & Sofia on a quiet evening.
- **Sound** — water, wind, leaves; the district's quietest place.
- **What slowly changes** — the bank shifts; the towpath wears; the oak sheds a
  limb once a decade.
- **What never changes** — the bridge; the oak; the waterline the flood never
  crosses.
- **Quiet mystery** — **this is the mystery's home.** The river keeps a
  double-tide that follows the two moons and not the rain; the oak leans toward
  the water and turns gold a night early; the bridge's waterline has never been
  crossed. None of it is ever explained.
- **What the player may never learn** — how old the oak is; what lies on the far
  bank (never reachable); why the river stays calm when the sky is not.
- **Never** — never explain the oak or the tides; never let the far bank be
  reached; never crowd this place with life — its power is its emptiness.
- **Visual direction** — cool, still, silver-green; the one hush in a warm
  district; the two moons often on the water.

## Museum Quarter — *memory*
- **Essence** — the district's deliberate memory; where it keeps what must not be
  forgotten.
- **Role** — Historic Memory made a place: quiet, reflective, slow.
- **Composition** — the club museum (Nora), the first-trophy case, the retired
  shirts, the sealed archive, hushed halls.
- **Daily rhythm** — a quiet opening, school tours, cataloguing, the display
  lights at night.
- **Match day** — the quietest place; a few come to remember before the noise.
- **Seasonal & weather** — anniversary exhibits; the museum marks what the
  calendar forgets.
- **Residents & life** — Nora, Victor's cabinet, Agnes as living archive,
  schoolchildren.
- **Sound** — footsteps, hush, the low hum of the cases.
- **What slowly changes** — the collection grows; new heroes join the walls; scars
  are kept, never erased.
- **What never changes** — the first trophy stays in the centre case.
- **Quiet mystery** — the museum's oldest photograph shows the same **two moons**
  over a stadium that isn't quite the current one. It is catalogued without comment.
- **What the player may never learn** — what is in the sealed archive; the story
  behind the season Nora will not display.
- **Never** — never rewrite history; never erase a scar; never turn memory into a
  leaderboard.
- **Visual direction** — cool lit cases in a warm district; stillness; reverence.

## The Academy — *hope*
- **Essence** — the district's future; where dreams are still allowed to be enormous.
- **Role** — hope: children, parents, the promise of the homegrown hero.
- **Composition** — the training pitches, the fence Victor watches from, small
  stands, muddy boots.
- **Daily rhythm** — quiet mornings; children after school; parents at the fence
  in the evening.
- **Match day** — the young watch the big ground and imagine.
- **Seasonal & weather** — the churned winter pitch; the bright, hopeful spring
  intake.
- **Residents & life** — the Twins, Victor at the fence, coaches; Luca once
  dreamed here.
- **Sound** — whistles, shouts, a ball on wet grass.
- **What slowly changes** — intakes come and go; one day one of the Twins walks in.
- **What never changes** — the fence; the dream.
- **Quiet mystery** — under a double-moon night the academy floodlight throws a
  faint *double shadow* of every child — twice the players on the pitch, for a
  moment. Never mentioned.
- **What the player may never learn** — which child will make it; who watched from
  the fence years ago.
- **Never** — never guarantee a graduate; never make hope transactional.
- **Visual direction** — green, muddy, hopeful; small figures against a big dream.

## The Railway — *arrival & departure*
- **Essence** — the district's threshold; where the world comes in and goes out.
- **Role** — arrival, departure, visitors, journalists, away supporters; the edge
  that connects to everywhere else.
- **Composition** — the station, Karim's kiosk, the platform, the line running out
  past the edge of the visible world.
- **Daily rhythm** — commuters at dawn; a quiet midday; the school crowd; the last
  train at night.
- **Match day** — overflowing: away supporters, journalists, the surge in and the
  tide out.
- **Seasonal & weather** — umbrellas, cold platforms, the breath of winter trains.
- **Residents & life** — Karim, arrivals and departures, Emma meeting a train.
- **Sound** — rails, announcements, the arrival roar.
- **What slowly changes** — the timetable; who arrives famous and who leaves
  forgotten.
- **What never changes** — Karim opens with the first train; the line always runs
  out past the frame.
- **Quiet mystery** — the line runs to the edge of the world and simply
  *continues*, into a soft distance no map shows; the last train each night goes
  somewhere no one names.
- **What the player may never learn** — where the line actually goes; the identity
  of the regular who only ever arrives and never leaves.
- **Never** — never show the line's destination; never let the edge feel like a wall.
- **Visual direction** — iron and steam at the district's threshold; the one place
  that points *outward*.
