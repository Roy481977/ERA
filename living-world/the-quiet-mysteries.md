# ERA — The Quiet Mysteries (the two moons)  _[CKO-proposed]_

**Status: PROPOSED.** The never-explained substrate of the district. Per Roy: the
story is borderline mystical — the *two moons* — and it must be attached to the
core story of the place, **but it is never talked about.** This document defines
that layer and, more importantly, the rule that keeps it silent.

---

## The rule that governs everything here

These things are **seen, never said.**

- No resident ever remarks on them. No child ever asks.
- There is no lore page, tutorial, menu entry, quest, achievement, or any in-game
  text that names or explains them.
- They grant no advantage and drive no mechanic.
- They exist only to be *noticed* — and a player may play for years, never
  consciously notice, and lose nothing.

This is the purest expression of the district's own laws: **~20–30% always beyond
view; design places before stories; the world whispers "come and look," never
"use me."** Philosophically it is **Wonder** (IP-001) and the deepest form of
**Independence** (IP-002, axiom AR): *a world that holds truths it will never
explain to you.* You are a guest in the district, not its author.

---

## The root fact of the setting

The district was designed, from the first stroke, **under two moons.** ERA is not
quite Earth. It is a football place that grew for over a century *somewhere just
beside the real* — close enough to ache for, not quite reachable. Everything else
descends from this un-named fact: the warmth of the light, the calm of the river,
the age of the oak, the particular quiet.

This is why the art direction is warm and painterly: the style is **diegetic**,
not decorative. The world looks slightly enchanted because it *is*.

---

## The mysteries (kept few, on purpose)

- **The two moons.** A small quick moon and a large slow one, their phases
  drifting in and out of sync. Most nights one hangs over the stadium. On rare
  nights both are full — a **double-moon night** — and the district is quietly,
  unaccountably more alive: the plaza fuller, the pub warmer, the songs longer.
  No one ever says why.
- **The river answers the moons, not the weather.** It keeps a gentle
  double-tide and has moods that follow the sky. The old stone bridge carries a
  waterline no flood has ever crossed.
- **The old oak.** Older than the district, older than football. It leans a
  fraction toward the river and turns gold a night before every other tree. No
  one planted it; no one remembers it young.
- **The long light.** The district's light runs a touch warmer and a touch longer
  than the hour should allow — a near-perpetual almost-evening.

---

## How it attaches to every place

Every place carries **one** thread of this and no more — see the *quiet mystery*
field in [`places.md`](places.md). The moons are in the sky above the stadium;
the river and oak are the mystery's home at the Riverside; the fountain holds two
reflections on a clear night; the museum's oldest photograph shows the same two
moons; the academy floodlight throws a faint double shadow; the railway line runs
out past the edge of the world into a distance no map shows. The threads are
never gathered and never explained. They simply make the place cohere at a level
below noticing.

---

## Never

- Never name or explain the two moons in any text, voice, tutorial, or menu.
- Never let a resident discuss them.
- Never turn them into a quest, collectible, or achievement.
- Never let them affect competitive or fantasy outcomes.
- Never over-seed them — a mystery explained everywhere is no mystery. One quiet
  thread per place.
- Never remove them. They are the root of the setting.
